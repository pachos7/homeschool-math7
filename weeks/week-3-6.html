Content

📚 What is an Inequality?

An inequality is a mathematical statement that compares two expressions using one of these symbols:

  • < — less than
  • > — greater than
  • ≤ — less than or equal to
  • ≥ — greater than or equal to

Unlike an equation (which has one answer), an inequality has infinitely many solutions.

✏️ Solving Inequalities

Solve inequalities just like equations — with one important rule:

⚠️ If you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number, flip the inequality sign!

Example 1 — One-step: x + 3 > 7

  1. Subtract 3 from both sides: x > 4
  2. Solution: all numbers greater than 4

Example 2 — Two-step: 2x − 1 ≤ 9

  1. Add 1 to both sides: 2x ≤ 10
  2. Divide both sides by 2: x ≤ 5
  3. Solution: all numbers less than or equal to 5

Example 3 — Negative division (sign flips!): −3x > 12

  1. Divide both sides by −3 and flip the sign: x < −4

📊 Graphing on a Number Line

  • Use an open circle (○) for < or > (the endpoint is NOT included)
  • Use a closed circle (●) for ≤ or ≥ (the endpoint IS included)
  • Shade the arrow in the direction of all solutions

💡 Real-World Connection

  • Speed limits: You must drive at a speed ≤ 60 km/h
  • Age restrictions: You must be ≥ 18 years old to vote
  • Budget planning: Monthly expenses must be < monthly income
  • Temperature safety: A refrigerator must stay ≤ 4°C

Challenges

  1. Write an inequality for: "A number is at least 12."
  2. Solve and graph: x − 4 > 3
  3. Solve and graph: 2x + 1 ≤ 11
  4. Solve and graph: −4x ≥ 20 (remember to flip the sign!)
  5. Solve and graph: 3x − 5 < x + 7
  6. Word Problem: You want to spend no more than $50 at a market. You already have fruit costing $12 and bread costing $8. Write and solve an inequality to find how much you have left to spend on other items.
  7. True or False: Is x = −5 a solution to 2x + 3 > −9? Show your work to justify your answer.
