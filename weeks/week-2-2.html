Key Concepts

Simple Interest is calculated only on the principal (original) amount:

Interest = Principal × Rate × Time

Example: $100 at 5% for 1 year = $100 × 0.05 × 1 = $5

Compound Interest is calculated on the principal plus any accumulated interest:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal (starting amount)
  • r = Annual interest rate (as a decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Time in years

Why Compound Interest Matters

Compound interest is often called "interest on interest." It's one of the most powerful concepts in finance because:

  • It helps your savings grow faster over time
  • It's used in savings accounts, investments, and retirement funds
  • It can also work against you with credit card debt and loans
  • Starting to save early makes a huge difference due to compounding
