Week 2.8: Understanding Mortgages

← Back to Part II: Global Economics and Investment

Objective

  • Understand what a mortgage is and how it works.
  • Learn about key mortgage components: loan amount, interest rate, loan term, and down payment.
  • Use real-world mortgage calculators to simulate different scenarios.
  • Analyze how changing variables affects monthly payments and total interest paid.

Tools

  • Calculator (physical or digital)
  • Bankrate Mortgage Calculator
  • Bancolombia Mortgage Simulator
  • BBVA Colombia Mortgage Simulator
  • Spreadsheet software for creating comparison tables

Content

🏠 Real-World Simulation

Featured House: $500,000,000 COP

View House Listing

Discussion Questions:

  • Is $500,000,000 COP a lot of money?
  • What does it mean to borrow that much?
  • How long would it take to save that amount?
  • Why do people need mortgages to buy houses?

💻 Mortgage Simulation Activity

Using the mortgage calculators listed in the Tools section, simulate a mortgage for the featured house with different scenarios:

Variables to Try:

  • Loan Terms: 10, 15, 20, and 30 years
  • Interest Rates: Use default rates or adjust if allowed (typical range: 8-15% in Colombia)
  • Down Payments: 20%, 30%, and 40%

Analysis Tasks:

  • Compare monthly payments across different scenarios
  • Calculate total interest paid over the life of each loan
  • Create a comparison table showing how loan term affects total cost
  • Create a comparison table showing how interest rate affects total cost
  • Determine which scenario results in the lowest total cost

🤔 Key Questions to Explore

  • How does the loan term affect monthly payments?
  • How does the loan term affect total interest paid?
  • Why might someone choose a 30-year mortgage over a 15-year mortgage, even if it costs more overall?
  • How much can you save by making a larger down payment?
  • What is the trade-off between monthly payment affordability and total cost?

💡 Real-World Connection

Understanding mortgages helps you:

  • Make informed decisions about one of the biggest purchases in life
  • Understand the long-term financial commitment of homeownership
  • Compare different loan options and choose the best one for your situation
  • Plan for major life goals involving significant borrowing

Challenges

  • Use a mortgage calculator to simulate the featured house ($500,000,000 COP) with at least 4 different scenarios
  • Create a detailed comparison table showing loan term, interest rate, down payment, monthly payment, and total interest for each scenario
  • Calculate the difference in total cost between a 15-year and 30-year mortgage for the same house
  • Determine how much money you would save in total interest by increasing your down payment from 20% to 30%
  • Family Interview: Ask your family members: "How did you decide how much to borrow when buying your home?" and "Did you use a bank simulator or talk to a financial advisor?"
  • Write a one-paragraph reflection: Based on your simulations, what mortgage strategy would you recommend to someone buying this house, and why?
