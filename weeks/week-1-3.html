Week 1.3: Ratios, Rates and Proportions

Objective

  • Understand and use ratios to compare quantities.
  • Differentiate between ratios, rates, and unit rates.
  • Apply ratios and rates to solve real-life problems.

Key Concepts

A Ratio compares two quantities. Examples:

  • 3:2 (apples to oranges)
  • 4 to 5 (boys to girls)
  • ½ (shorthand for ratio)

A Rate is a ratio that compares two different units. Examples:

  • 50 miles per hour
  • $2.50 for 5 apples
  • 6 Gallons per minute

TIP: Always label units when working with rates and ratios

Practice Scenarios

Scenario 1. Given the following promotion, which option would you choose?

  • 12 sodas for $6
  • 18 sodas for $9

Scenario 2 – Speed and Travel

Example: A train travels 240 km in 3 hours.

Ask: What is the speed in km/h? If it continues for 5 hours, how far will it go?

Scenario 3 – Recipes & Cooking

If 4 people need 2 cups of rice, how many cups are needed for 10 people?

Challenges

  • A car uses 15 gallons of gas to go 300 miles. What's the miles per gallon?
  • You buy 3 pens for $1.20. What's the unit rate per pen?
  • A smoothie recipe uses 2 bananas for every 3 cups of milk. How many bananas for 9 cups of milk?
