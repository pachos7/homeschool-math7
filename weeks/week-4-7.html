Basic Probability

Probability is the mathematical way of calculating how likely an event is to happen. It is expressed as a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain). You can also write probability as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.

  • Probability Formula: P(Event) = (Number of Favorable Outcomes) / (Total Number of Possible Outcomes)
  • Example: When rolling a standard 6-sided die, what is the probability of rolling a 4? There is only one 4 (favorable outcome) and 6 total sides (possible outcomes), so the probability is 1/6.

Challenges

  1. Probability Experiment:

    Grab a standard coin and flip it 20 times. Keep a tally of how many times it lands on "Heads" and how many times it lands on "Tails".

    • What is the theoretical probability of flipping a "Heads"? (Hint: Use the probability formula above).
    • What was your experimental probability? (The number of times you actually got Heads divided by 20).
    • Did your experimental probability match the theoretical probability exactly? Why or why not?
