Content

📚 What is Algebra?

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that uses letters and symbols to represent numbers and quantities in formulas and equations. Unlike arithmetic, which deals with specific numbers, algebra allows us to work with unknowns and solve problems in a more general way.

💡 Algebra vs. Arithmetic

Key Differences:

  • Arithmetic: Works with specific numbers (e.g., 5 + 3 = 8)
  • Algebra: Works with variables and unknowns (e.g., x + 3 = 8, solve for x)
  • Arithmetic: Answers specific questions
  • Algebra: Creates formulas that can solve many similar problems at once

🌍 Real-World Connection

Algebra helps us:

  • Solve problems where we don't know all the information yet
  • Create formulas for repeated calculations
  • Understand patterns and relationships
  • Make predictions based on known information
  • Model real-world situations mathematically
