Week 2.9: Certificate Deposits (CDs)

← Back to Part II: Global Economics and Investment

Objective

  • Understand what Certificate Deposits (CDs) are and how they work.
  • Learn about compound interest and different compounding terms.
  • Compare CD interest rates between different countries (Colombia vs. US).
  • Analyze how different variables affect total interest gains.
  • Explore simple investment instruments for safe savings.

Tools

  • Calculator (physical or digital)
  • US Investor.gov Compound Interest Calculator
  • Colombia CD Simulators:
    • Banco Falabella CDT Simulator
    • Santander CDT Simulator
  • US CD Calculators:
    • Fidelity Banker CD Calculator
  • Spreadsheet software for creating comparison tables

💰 Real-World CD Simulation

Starting Amount: 1,000,000 COP

Time Period: 1 year

Focus: Understanding compound interest with different compounding terms

Variables to Try:

  • Compounding Terms:
    • Daily compounding
    • Monthly compounding
    • Semester (semi-annual) compounding
    • Yearly compounding
  • Interest Rates: Compare average rates between Colombia and the US
    • Use the simulators to find current Colombian CD rates
    • Use the US calculators to compare American CD rates
    • Note the differences and discuss why they might exist

Analysis Tasks:

  • Compare total interest gains across different compounding frequencies
  • Create a comparison table showing how changing the term affects total gain
  • Create a comparison table showing how changing the interest rate affects total gain
  • Calculate the difference in earnings between daily and yearly compounding
  • Compare returns between Colombia and US CD rates

🤔 Key Questions to Explore

  • What is a Certificate Deposit and how does it differ from a regular savings account?
  • Why do CDs typically offer higher interest rates than savings accounts?
  • How does the compounding frequency affect your total earnings?
  • What are the risks and benefits of investing in CDs?
  • Why might CD interest rates differ between Colombia and the US?
  • What happens if you need to withdraw money from a CD before it matures?
  • What other simple investment instruments exist besides CDs?

💡 Real-World Connection

Understanding CDs helps you:

  • Make informed decisions about safe investment options
  • Understand the power of compound interest over time
  • Compare different investment vehicles for your savings goals
  • Learn about risk-free or low-risk investment strategies
  • Build financial literacy for future money management

Challenges

  • Use at least two CD simulators (one from Colombia, one from the US) to calculate returns on 1,000,000 COP for 1 year
  • Create a detailed comparison table showing:
    • Different compounding frequencies (daily, monthly, semester, yearly)
    • Interest earned for each scenario
    • Total amount at the end of the term
  • Calculate the percentage difference in earnings between daily and yearly compounding
  • Compare the average CD interest rates between Colombia and the US - create a chart showing the difference
  • Research and list 3 other simple, low-risk investment instruments besides CDs
  • Family Engagement: Ask your family: "Can I open a CD, or review with you the next CD you open?"
  • Write a one-paragraph reflection: Based on your simulations, would you recommend a CD as a good investment option for saving money? Why or why not?
