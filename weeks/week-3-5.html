Week 3.5: Multi-Step Equations and Variables on Both Sides

← Back to Part III: Algebra and Geometry Foundations

Objective

  • Solve equations that require more than one step to isolate the variable.
  • Solve equations where the variable appears on both sides of the equal sign.
  • Apply the distributive property to simplify equations before solving.
  • Check solutions by substituting back into the original equation.
  • Translate real-world problems into multi-step equations and solve them.

Tools

  • Notebook and pencil for step-by-step work
  • Calculator (for checking answers only)

Content

📚 Key Concept: Multi-Step Equations

A multi-step equation requires you to perform two or more operations to isolate the variable. The goal is always to get the variable alone on one side.

Strategy — work in this order:

  1. Distribute (if needed)
  2. Combine like terms on each side
  3. Move variable terms to one side
  4. Move constant terms to the other side
  5. Divide (or multiply) to solve for the variable
  6. Check your answer!

✏️ Example: Simple Multi-Step

Solve: 2x + 5 = 17

  1. Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 12
  2. Divide both sides by 2: x = 6
  3. Check: 2(6) + 5 = 17 ✓

✏️ Example: Variables on Both Sides

Solve: 5x − 3 = 2x + 9

  1. Subtract 2x from both sides: 3x − 3 = 9
  2. Add 3 to both sides: 3x = 12
  3. Divide both sides by 3: x = 4
  4. Check: 5(4) − 3 = 17 and 2(4) + 9 = 17 ✓

✏️ Example: With Distributive Property

Solve: 3(x + 4) = 2x + 17

  1. Distribute: 3x + 12 = 2x + 17
  2. Subtract 2x from both sides: x + 12 = 17
  3. Subtract 12 from both sides: x = 5
  4. Check: 3(5 + 4) = 27 and 2(5) + 17 = 27 ✓

💡 Real-World Connection

  • Splitting a restaurant bill fairly among friends requires multi-step thinking
  • Comparing two phone plans to find when they cost the same
  • Figuring out how many hours to work to save a specific amount of money

Challenges

  1. Solve and check: 3x + 7 = 22
  2. Solve and check: 4x − 5 = 2x + 9
  3. Solve and check: 6x + 1 = 3x + 16
  4. Solve and check: 5(x − 2) = 3x + 4
  5. Saving money: Sofia and Manuel are saving money. Sofia has $15 and saves $8 per week. Manuel has $45 and saves $4 per week. After how many weeks will they have the same amount? Write and solve an equation.
  6. Movie tickets: A school club is raising money by selling tickets to a movie night. They spent $120 to rent the movie and snacks. Each ticket they sell costs $8. The club wants to earn $200 in profit after covering the $120 cost. How many tickets must the club sell to reach their goal?
  7. Ollivanders Wand Shop purchase: Each wand costs 7 Galleons and each spell book costs 4 Galleons. A student buys 3 spell books and some wands. The total bill is 46 Galleons. Question: How many wands did the student buy?
