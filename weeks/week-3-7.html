Week 3.7: Geometric Figures — Angles, Triangles, and Constructions

← Back to Part III: Algebra and Geometry Foundations

Objective

  • Identify and classify triangles by their sides (scalene, isosceles, equilateral) and angles (acute, right, obtuse).
  • Understand the Triangle Angle Sum property: all interior angles add up to 180°.
  • Learn basic geometric constructions using a compass and ruler.
  • Identify and classify quadrilaterals and other polygons.
  • Apply geometric properties to solve missing angle problems.

Tools

  • Compass and ruler
  • Protractor
  • Pencil and paper
  • Notebook for notes and geometric sketches

Content

🔺 Types of Triangles — By Sides

  • Equilateral: All 3 sides equal, all angles = 60°
  • Isosceles: 2 sides equal, 2 base angles equal
  • Scalene: No sides equal, no angles equal

🔺 Types of Triangles — By Angles

  • Acute triangle: All three angles less than 90°
  • Right triangle: One angle exactly 90°
  • Obtuse triangle: One angle greater than 90°

📐 Triangle Angle Sum

The three interior angles of any triangle always add up to 180°.

Example: A triangle has angles of 55° and 72°. Find the third angle.
Third angle = 180° − 55° − 72° = 53°

🔷 Quadrilaterals

  • Square: 4 equal sides, 4 right angles
  • Rectangle: 2 pairs of equal sides, 4 right angles
  • Parallelogram: 2 pairs of parallel sides, opposite angles equal
  • Rhombus: 4 equal sides, opposite angles equal
  • Trapezoid: Exactly one pair of parallel sides

The interior angles of any quadrilateral always add up to 360°.

🧭 Basic Constructions (Compass & Ruler)

  • Bisecting an angle: Divide an angle into two equal halves
  • Perpendicular bisector: A line that crosses the midpoint of a segment at 90°
  • Constructing an equilateral triangle: Use a compass set to the length of one side

💡 Real-World Connection

  • Architects and engineers use triangles because they are the strongest geometric shape
  • Bridges and roof trusses rely on triangular structures for stability
  • Artists use geometric shapes and constructions in design and perspective drawing
  • Navigation and surveying use angle-finding techniques derived from triangle geometry

Challenges

  1. Classify each triangle: (a) sides 5 cm, 5 cm, 5 cm; (b) angles 90°, 45°, 45°; (c) sides 3 cm, 5 cm, 7 cm.
  2. Find the missing angle in a triangle with angles of 48° and 67°.
  3. A quadrilateral has angles of 90°, 85°, and 110°. Find the fourth angle.
  4. Using a compass and ruler, construct an equilateral triangle with a side length of 5 cm. Measure the angles with a protractor to verify they are each 60°.
  5. Using a compass and ruler, bisect a 70° angle. Verify each half measures 35° with your protractor.
  6. Observation Challenge: Go outside or look around your home and find at least 3 examples of triangles in real structures (roofs, bridges, furniture, etc.). Sketch them and try to classify each one.
  7. Algebra + Geometry: A triangle has angles of x°, (x + 15)°, and (2x − 5)°. Write and solve an equation to find the value of x, then find all three angles.
